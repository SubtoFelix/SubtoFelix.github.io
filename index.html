On the Creation of Great Comics and Animations

“The creation of a single world comes from a huge number of fragments and chaos.”
- Hayao Miyasaki -

Ever since I was very young, I have always love the adventure of the main character in a story filled with colourful settings and exciting plotline, whether in comics or animations. The way it brings out our childhood dream and imagination to life is what makes them so magical.

Of course, many people love differnt kinds of stories and have their own opinions of what is considered "marvelous" and "terrible".

Personally, I love watching and reading fantasy, mystery, and drama genres,but others might like romance and horror. Despite all that, there are some elements that can make an animation more captivating or a comic that engages more with the readers.

Without further ado, let's dive straight into them!

The Flow


If you don't understand what the comic or animation is about, you will probably not want to stick to them for too long. A confusing story will not attract any viewer. The flow of a great comic or animation has to be smooth and easy for the viewers to follow so they know what's going on.

Colours & Sound Effects


One of the most important factors that readers consider in choosing a comic to read is the arts. If the colours and drawings of a comic are aesthetically pleasing, readers are more likely to read it. The same goes for animations, except now, sounds are involved: if the colours and sound-effects convey a dramatic effect, the watchers will be more immersed in the story.

Characters


Have your ever felt strong sympathy or hatred for a character, despite them being fictional? Building characters that are unique and realistic will help foster connections between the viewers and the characters. Giving them flaws and strengths, interesting goals and motivations, along with hobbies and mannerisms will absorb the viewers into the story.

Storyline


A must for every good story is a consistent storyline. A story with clear focus that doesn't deviate from the main plot will keep the viewers engage and prevent them from getting comfused. Conflict and Tension is also a must in keeping them at the edge of their seats.

Worldbuilding


A fascinating, novel, and consistent worldbuilding will not only keep your readers glue to the screen (or paper), but also make your story stand out. Especially in a fantasy genre, the better the worldbuilding, the more likely the stories will attract more viewers and make them stick to the stories.
